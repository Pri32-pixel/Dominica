REQUEST FOR APPROVAL OF CONTRACTUAL EMPLOYMENT FOR THOMAS GILBERT AS PERMANENT SECRETARY – AGRICULTURE, FISHERIES, BLUE AND GREEN ECONOMY

﻿﻿I. BACKGROUND

The primary objective of this Cabinet Paper is to propose a comprehensive strategy for addressing the growing issue of urban congestion in major metropolitan areas. The memorandum aims to:

  1. Provide Cabinet members with a clear understanding of the current state of urban congestion and its detrimental impacts on economic productivity, environmental sustainability, and quality of life.
  2. Present a detailed analysis of the underlying causes contributing to urban congestion, including population growth, inadequate transportation infrastructure, inefficient land use patterns, and reliance on single-occupancy vehicles.
  3. Evaluate a range of potential policy interventions and transportation solutions aimed at alleviating congestion, enhancing mobility, and promoting sustainable urban development.

II. PROGRESS REPORT / RELEVANT INFORMATION

An analysis conducted by the AGRICULTURE, FISHERIES, BLUE AND GREEN ECONOMY has identified several key challenges facing secretariat hiring practices, including:

      1. Skill Shortages: There is a shortage of individuals with the specialized skills and competencies required to effectively perform secretariat duties, such as administrative proficiency, communication skills, analytical abilities, and familiarity with relevant software tools and systems.
      2. Attrition Rates: High turnover rates among secretariat staff pose a significant challenge to continuity and institutional memory within government departments. The loss of experienced personnel can disrupt operations, delay projects, and impede the achievement of organizational objectives.
      3. Diversity and Inclusion: Ensuring diversity and inclusion within the secretariat workforce remains a priority for the government. However, efforts to attract and retain a diverse talent pool have been hampered by systemic barriers, unconscious biases, and limited outreach and recruitment initiatives targeting underrepresented groups

III. CONCLUSION

In conclusion, the recommendations outlined in this cabinet paper present a strategic framework for optimizing secretariat contractual hiring practices within government ministries and departments. By embracing flexibility, innovation, and Upload scanned signatureability in the procurement and management of external service providers, the government can enhance its capacity to deliver high-quality secretariat support services, respond effectively to changing organizational needs, and achieve value for money.

The proposed recommendations emphasize the importance of strategic outsourcing, contractual agility, competitive procurement practices, performance monitoring, risk management, and capacity building in maximizing the benefits of secretariat contractual arrangements while mitigating associated risks and challenges. By adopting a proactive and systematic approach to contractual hiring, the government can leverage external expertise, foster innovation, and enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of service delivery across the public sector.

IV. INVITATION TO CABINET

The Cabinet is therefore invited to consider and advise approval for the following:

Approval for contractual employment of Thomas Gilbert as Permanent Secretary, Agriculture, Fisheries, Blue and Green Economy, for a period of two years under the following terms and conditions: 

Salary:                                        XCD 000.00 per month 

Vacation Leave:                         Twenty – one (21) working days per annum 

Gratuity:                                     Twenty percent (20%) of basic salary

Cabinet Paper for Creating Policies for Marine Pollution Prevention and Control

Cabinet Paper by the Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries, Blue and Green Economy


Marine pollution poses a significant threat to the health of our oceans, coastal ecosystems, and marine life. It is imperative that we take proactive measures to prevent and control marine pollution to safeguard our environment and sustainably manage our marine resources. In light of this, the following Cabinet Paper outlines proposed policies for addressing marine pollution:

  1. Shall the Cabinet establish a comprehensive regulatory framework to govern marine pollution prevention and control activities
  2. Shall the Cabinet conduct a comprehensive assessment to identify major sources of marine pollution, including industrial discharges, agricultural runoff, sewage outfalls, and maritime activities?
  3. Decision Point: Shall the Cabinet develop and implement pollution prevention strategies targeting key sources of marine pollution.
